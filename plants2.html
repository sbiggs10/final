Dry Climates

Abelia

The Abelia is a genus of flowering shrubs known for their attractive, semi-evergreen foliage and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom from spring to fall. Native to East Asia and Mexico, they are typically easy to grow, making them popular in gardens and landscapes. Abelias prefer well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. They are drought-tolerant once established and can grow between 3 to 6 feet tall depending on the variety. The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Abelias are also deer-resistant and low-maintenance.

Liriope

Liriope is a low-maintenance, evergreen perennial plant commonly used in landscaping as ground cover or edging. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is known for its grass-like appearance, with clumps of arching, strap-shaped leaves. Liriope produces small, lavender or white flower spikes in late summer, which are followed by dark berries. It thrives in a variety of soil conditions and is tolerant of both sun and shade, though it prefers partial shade. Liriope is drought-resistant and easy to care for, making it a popular choice for garden borders, erosion control, and as an ornamental feature.

Barberry

The Barberry plant (Berberis species) is a hardy, deciduous or evergreen shrub known for its sharp thorns, vibrant foliage, and small, red or yellow berries. Native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it's commonly grown for ornamental purposes due to its colorful leaves, which range from green to deep red or purple, depending on the variety. Barberry bushes can grow in a range of soil types and are often used in hedging or as a landscape accent.

Barberry is drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, thriving in full sun or partial shade. However, some species, particularly the invasive Japanese Barberry (Berberis thunbergii), are known to outcompete native plants and create favorable environments for ticks, so they are controlled or banned in certain regions. The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Texas Sage

Texas Sage (Leucophyllum frutescens), also known as Cenizo or Barometer Bush, is a hardy, evergreen shrub native to Texas and northern Mexico. It thrives in hot, dry climates and is drought-tolerant, making it popular in xeriscaping. Texas Sage features silvery-gray foliage and blooms profusely with purple, pink, or white flowers, usually after rain or during high humidity. It prefers well-draining soil and full sunlight, and is low-maintenance, requiring minimal water once established. Its compact size, attractive appearance, and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies make it a favorite in landscaping.

Perennials

Hibiscus

The Hibiscus is a genus of flowering plants known for its large, colorful blooms. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, hibiscus plants are popular in gardens for their striking flowers, which come in a variety of colors including red, pink, yellow, and white. They typically bloom in the summer and fall. Hibiscus flowers are often used to make teas, such as hibiscus tea, which is rich in antioxidants and has potential health benefits like lowering blood pressure.

The plant requires full sunlight, well-drained soil, and regular watering to thrive. It's commonly grown as an ornamental plant but can also be used for medicinal purposes and in beauty products. Some species, like Hibiscus rosa-sinensis, are famous for their beauty, while others, such as Hibiscus sabdariffa, are cultivated for culinary and health uses.

Ruellia simplex

Ruellia simplex, commonly known as Mexican petunia or Mexican bluebell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico, the Caribbean, and South America. It’s admired for its striking purple, pink, or white trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom prolifically, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant features long, narrow, dark green leaves and is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Ruellia simplex is known for its hardiness, thriving in a variety of conditions including full sun or partial shade, and is both drought-tolerant and adaptable to wet soils. However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its aggressive spread via seeds and underground runners.

In some areas, particularly in the southeastern United States, it’s classified as an invasive species, so gardeners should take care to manage its growth. Despite its invasive potential, Ruellia simplex is popular for its vibrant flowers and ease of care.

Coneflowers

Coneflowers (Echinacea) are hardy, perennial flowering plants native to North America. Known for their daisy-like appearance, they feature large, vibrant blooms with raised, cone-shaped centers surrounded by drooping petals. Coneflowers are often found in shades of pink, purple, white, and orange.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular in gardens for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Coneflowers are drought-tolerant, low-maintenance, and bloom from early summer to fall. They also have medicinal properties, often used to boost the immune system in herbal remedies.

Lantana

Lantana is a genus of flowering plants in the verbena family, native to tropical regions of the Americas and Africa. Known for its vibrant, multicolored flowers that change shades as they age, Lantana is popular in gardens and landscaping. It thrives in sunny environments and is drought-tolerant, making it ideal for warm climates. Lantana attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees but can be invasive in some areas due to its fast growth. While beautiful, its leaves and unripe berries are toxic to humans and pets if ingested. Regular pruning helps manage its spread.

Annuals

Petunia

Petunias are popular flowering plants known for their vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms and wide range of colors. Native to South America, they are commonly grown as annuals in gardens, hanging baskets, and containers. Petunias thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, blooming from spring to fall. There are four main types: Grandiflora (large flowers), Multiflora (smaller, more abundant flowers), Milliflora (tiny blooms), and Spreading or Wave petunias (low-growing, spreading habit). Petunias are relatively easy to care for, requiring regular watering, deadheading to promote continuous blooming, and occasional fertilization.

Pentas

Pentas (Pentas lanceolata), also known as Egyptian Star Cluster, is a tropical plant popular for its star-shaped flowers and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds. It blooms in vibrant colors such as red, pink, lavender, and white. Pentas are typically grown as perennials in warm climates or annuals in cooler regions.

The plant thrives in full sun, well-drained soil, and warm temperatures, making it ideal for gardens, borders, or containers. It requires regular watering but can tolerate drought once established. Pentas are also low-maintenance and heat-tolerant, making them perfect for adding long-lasting color to outdoor spaces.

Zinnias

Zinnias are bright, hardy, and easy-to-grow flowering plants native to Mexico and Central America. They are known for their vibrant, daisy-like blooms that come in a wide range of colors, including red, orange, pink, yellow, and white. Zinnias thrive in warm climates and require full sunlight and well-drained soil. They are popular in gardens due to their ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees. Zinnias bloom from summer to early fall and are great for cut flowers. They are low-maintenance but sensitive to frost, making them ideal for annual planting.

Moss Rose

The Moss Rose (scientific name: Portulaca grandiflora) is a low-growing, succulent annual plant known for its vibrant, colorful flowers and ability to thrive in hot, dry conditions. Native to South America, it’s commonly used as ground cover in gardens due to its drought tolerance and ability to spread quickly.

Moss Roses feature fleshy, needle-like leaves and blooms that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, yellow, orange, and white. The flowers typically open in sunlight and close at night or during cloudy weather. They require well-drained soil and full sun to flourish, making them a popular choice for rock gardens, borders, and containers.

This plant is also relatively low-maintenance, attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, and can grow in poor soil, which adds to its appeal for gardeners looking for hardy, vibrant ground cover.
